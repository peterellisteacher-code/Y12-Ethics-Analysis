SOCRATIC METHOD

5 Steps of the Socratic Method:

Step 1: Find a statement. This can be a statement commonly accepted as truth.

Step 2: Find an exception. Try to think of situations or contexts where the statement would not be true. 

Step 3: If any exceptions are found, the statement is false or imprecise.

Step 4: Nuance the original statement to take into account any exceptions. 

Step 5: Repeat the process to discover any problems with the revised statement. You should end up with a statement that comes as close as possible to a statement of truth. It is by finding out what something is not that one comes closest to understanding what it is.
